Contemporary Works


Vega

2009

This original 5-movement piece is based on a true story about a stray cat (portrayed by the clarinet) that appeared at my doorstep in the summer of 2009. In this retelling the human (flute) cares for the cat in hopes of gaining her trust, while the human's pre-existing cats (the violin and cello) look upon the stray in disdain for invading their territory. The cat was named Vega after the brightest star in the constellation Lyra. Using the name as an influence on pitch material for this piece, I mapped musical intervals onto each constellation in the summer triangle to derive a unique harmonic language for each instrument.

Performers:
Emily Wespiser, flute; Devon LePore, clarinet; Isaac Shiman, violin; Brooks Griffith, cello;
Performed at the Ithaca College Fall 2009 Student Composition Recital
